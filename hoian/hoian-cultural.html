Old Town Hoi An

As one of the second-busiest port in Southeast Asia 16th century, Hoi An is known as a place to exchange goods of the traders Japan, China, Thailand, India. The remote merchant ships in Europe such as the Netherlands, Portugal, United Kingdom ... also visited.

You can choose to walk or rent a bike, or sitting on a cyclo to hear the driver both free access cycling after our events or other prominent monuments of Hoi An.

Everywhere in Hoi An, where you will also encounter friendly eyes, the smile of the people as the host welcomes guests. They welcome visitors, invited guests to buy souvenir items very polite and courteous. Absolutely no pictures unsettled, begging or clinging tourists.

Sitting just outside the the midday sweltering, you will witness the daily life of people here: those bear fruit vendors, vending tea, the boys and girls students after school hours cycling or image you sanitation workers despite the harsh sun to clean up the town every day. You will feel the peaceful life in Hoi An is simple.

To Hoi An full moon, you will enjoy the party "road motorcycle without sound, the brilliant hanging lanterns". Lantern is a very specific feature of Hoi An. Beautiful silk Hoi An, this is used to form the colorful lanterns make the town more vibrant nightlife as sparkling. Custom decorative lighting lanterns Hoi An full moon in the night from three centuries ago and still preserve continues to this day.

And travel, be sure to mention the food. Hoi An cuisine does not have a lot of rich food but with a few specialties, guests eat and will not be forgotten. Chicken rice is flexible soft rice dishes are served with shredded chicken little laksa leaves and onion. High floor, typical pasta in Hoi An very little water, meat, fried wontons with a little salad and prices will make you both switch panels and want to eat more.
